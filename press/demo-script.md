# playdrawr — Demo Script

Use this for live demos, video walkthroughs, or screen recordings.

---

## Setup (before the demo)
- Open playdrawr.co.uk in a browser
- Have playdrawr.co.uk/s/demo2026 ready in another tab
- Make the browser window full screen

---

## The Script

### 1. Homepage (30 seconds)
*Open playdrawr.co.uk*

"This is playdrawr — a free World Cup 2026 sweepstake tool. Creating a sweepstake takes about 30 seconds. But before I show you that, let me show you what it looks like from your participants' side — because that's the bit that matters most."

---

### 2. Participant view — Leaderboard (1 minute)
*Open playdrawr.co.uk/s/demo2026*

"This is what your participants see when you share the link. No login. No app. Just a link.

You can see the leaderboard — everyone's name, their team, their points. It updates automatically as matches are played. Right now you can see it's based on some sample results from the group stage.

Notice the points guide at the top — that explains how scoring works. Three points for a group stage win, up to 25 for winning the World Cup."

---

### 3. Fixtures tab (30 seconds)
*Click Fixtures tab*

"Here's every match in the tournament — kickoff times, scores, results. And you can see whose team is playing — the participant's name sits under the team. So you always know who to cheer for. Or against."

---

### 4. Groups tab (30 seconds)
*Click Groups tab*

"Full group stage standings. Updated in real time as results come in. The green bar marks the top two — the ones who qualify. You can see which groups are completed and which are still playing."

---

### 5. Organiser view (1 minute)
*Click 'Organiser view' in the demo bar at the top*

"This is the organiser dashboard — what you see when you're running the sweepstake. You can see who's paid their entry fee, manage participants, see the draw results.

You can edit participant names — click the pencil icon. Toggle paid status. It's all here."

*Click the Draw tab*

"And here's the draw view. You can see who drew which team. But the really fun bit is the draw itself — let me show you that."

*Click 'Replay draw'*

"This is the animated draw. Each team comes up one by one — the flag, the team name, and the person who drew them. You'd run this live with your group on a screen. Give it a second..."

*Let a few teams reveal*

"You can speed it up or skip to the end. In a real sweepstake you'd run this with everyone watching — it's actually quite tense."

---

### 6. Creating a sweepstake (1 minute)
*Go back to playdrawr.co.uk, click 'Start your draw'*

"Creating your own is simple. Sign up — free, just an email address. Then you're straight into the dashboard.

New sweepstake — give it a name, set an entry fee if you want one, choose how the payout works, and you're done. Then add your participants — name and email address. If you add their email, they get their team sent to them automatically when the draw is done.

Then hit the draw button. The animation runs, assignments save, emails go out. That's it."

## Key Points to Land

1. **Free** — say it twice. It's genuinely free, no catch.
2. **No login for participants** — biggest friction remover.
3. **The draw animation** — this is the differentiator. It's fun. Show it.
4. **Automatic scoring** — zero admin after draw day.
5. **One link** — share it on WhatsApp and you're done.

---

## Likely Questions

**"Is it really free?"**
Yes. No paid plans, no percentage of the prize pot, no catch.

**"What if someone joins after the draw?"**
The organiser can manually reassign teams, rebalance automatically, or run a full redraw with everyone included.

**"Does it work on mobile?"**
Yes — the participant view is fully responsive. Works on any device, any browser.

**"What happens when the tournament ends?"**
The leaderboard shows final standings. You settle up however you normally would — we track the prize pot but don't handle the money.

**"Can I use it for other tournaments?"**
Right now it's built specifically for World Cup 2026. More tournaments may come later.
